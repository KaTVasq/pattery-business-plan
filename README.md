# Pattery Business Plan

Business case study for **Pattery**, a technology-based service designed to provide portable battery rentals through a network of urban charging stations.

The project was developed as a group assignment for the **Business Creation** elective course. It combines market analysis, technical planning, marketing strategy, risk assessment and financial feasibility.

## Project overview

Pattery proposes a network of charging stations located across the city where users can rent portable batteries for minutes, hours or days.

The service is designed to be accessible through:

- A mobile application for iOS and Android
- A web platform
- Physical charging stations distributed across the city

The initial launch was planned for Madrid, with future expansion to other Spanish cities.

## Value proposition

Pattery addresses a common problem: running out of battery while away from home.

The service offers:

- Portable battery rental on demand
- Flexible rental periods
- Battery pickup and return at different stations
- Availability through mobile and web platforms
- A shared-use model focused on convenience and sustainability
- Loyalty rewards and promotional discounts

## Business model

The proposed pricing model includes:

- **Per minute:** €0.05
- **Per hour:** €1.50
- **Per day:** €5.00

The business plan also considers customer acquisition campaigns, referral rewards, seasonal discounts and a points-based loyalty programme.

## Market analysis

The project includes:

- Identification of the target audience
- Analysis of smartphone usage habits
- Study of customer needs
- Direct and indirect competitor comparison
- Market opportunity assessment
- Geographic expansion strategy

The main target segment is composed of users between 16 and 34 years old who spend significant time away from home and may not carry a charger or portable battery.

## Marketing strategy

The proposed marketing plan includes:

- Social media advertising
- Advertising in public transport locations
- Partnerships with events and organisations
- Referral discounts
- Loyalty programmes
- Newsletter campaigns
- Brand visibility through batteries and charging stations

The proposed slogan is:

> “Di adiós a los días sin energía con Pattery.”

## Technical feasibility

The technical plan considers:

- Portable batteries
- Urban charging stations
- Mobile applications for Android and iOS
- A responsive web platform
- MySQL database
- Servers and backend infrastructure
- User authentication
- Battery booking and rental
- Payment management
- Notifications
- Rental history
- Customer support
- Station geolocation

## Risk and contingency planning

The business plan evaluates several operational risks, including:

- Battery theft or non-return
- Damage to charging stations
- Low user adoption
- Excessive demand
- Server failures
- Cyberattacks
- Staff turnover

For each risk, the project proposes mitigation and contingency measures.

## Financial analysis

The study includes:

- Initial investment
- Fixed annual costs
- Revenue streams
- Break-even analysis
- Expected scenario
- Optimistic scenario
- Conservative scenario

The estimated initial investment was **€264,587.80**, including company creation, software development, charging stations, batteries, marketing, salaries, insurance and operational costs.
